# Law No. 7518 Analysis: Turkey's Crypto Asset Law

*Effective: July 2024*

## Overview

Law No. 7518 ("Kripto Varlık Hizmet Sağlayıcıları Hakkında Kanun") establishes Turkey's comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto asset service providers (CASPs). This represents Turkey's first dedicated crypto legislation, moving beyond previous piecemeal regulations.

## Key Definitions

### Crypto Asset (Kripto Varlık)
- Intangible assets created virtually using distributed ledger technology
- Capable of being transferred, stored, or traded electronically
- Does NOT include central bank digital currencies

### Crypto Asset Service Provider (CASP)
- Platforms enabling crypto trading, custody, transfer
- Wallet providers
- Exchange services between crypto and fiat

### Platform (Platform)
- Electronic systems facilitating crypto asset transactions
- Must be licensed by CMB

## Regulatory Authority

### Capital Markets Board (CMB/SPK)
**Primary Regulator:**
- Licensing and supervision of CASPs
- Prudential requirements
- Consumer protection standards
- Market integrity rules

### MASAK (Financial Intelligence Unit)
**AML/CFT Authority:**
- Anti-money laundering requirements
- Counter-terrorism financing
- Suspicious transaction reporting
- Travel Rule implementation

## Licensing Requirements

### License Categories
1. **Platform License** - Operating a crypto trading platform
2. **Wallet Service License** - Custody and wallet services
3. **Transfer Service License** - Crypto transfer services

### Application Requirements
- Minimum capital requirements (varies by license type)
- Fit and proper assessment for shareholders/managers
- IT security and operational resilience plans
- AML/CFT program documentation
- Customer complaint handling procedures

### Capital Requirements (Estimated)
| License Type | Minimum Capital (TRY) |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Platform | 10,000,000 |
| Wallet | 5,000,000 |
| Transfer | 2,500,000 |

*Note: Final requirements subject to CMB communiqués*

## Customer Protection

### Segregation Requirements
- Customer assets MUST be segregated from platform assets
- Prohibition on using customer assets for platform operations
- Independent custody arrangements required

### Insurance/Guarantee
- Platforms must maintain insurance or guarantees
- Protection against theft, hacking, operational failures

### Information Requirements
- Clear risk disclosures
- Fee transparency
- Transaction confirmations

## AML/CFT Obligations

### Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Identity verification for all customers
- Source of funds verification for large transactions
- Ongoing monitoring

### Suspicious Transaction Reporting
- Report to MASAK within 10 business days
- No tipping off permitted
- Record retention: 8 years

### Travel Rule (effective February 2025)
- Originator/beneficiary information for transfers >15,000 TRY
- Real-time transmission to receiving CASP
- Reject transfers lacking required information

## Prohibited Activities

### For CASPs
- Using customer assets as collateral
- Providing credit/leverage to retail customers (restrictions)
- Market manipulation
- Front-running customer orders

### General Prohibitions
- Anonymous crypto transactions (for CASPs)
- Operating without license (criminal penalty)
- False advertising of crypto services

## Enforcement

### Administrative Sanctions
- Fines up to 500,000 TRY per violation
- License suspension or revocation
- Public warnings

### Criminal Penalties
- Unlicensed operation: Up to 5 years imprisonment
- Fraud/embezzlement: Aggravated penalties
- AML violations: Referral to MASAK

## Transitional Provisions

### Existing Operators
- 6-month grace period to apply for license
- Must demonstrate ongoing compliance during transition
- Mandatory audits before license issuance

### Timeline
- Law effective: July 2024
- CMB implementing regulations: November 2024
- License application deadline: January 2025
- Full compliance required: July 2025

## Comparison with Previous Regulations

### Before Law 7518
- No dedicated crypto law
- Central Bank payment prohibition (April 2021) - crypto for payments banned
- MASAK AML requirements applied generally
- No licensing framework

### After Law 7518
- Comprehensive licensing regime
- Clear regulatory authority (CMB)
- Customer protection standards
- Market integrity rules

## Implications for CASPs

### Immediate Actions Required
1. Register with CMB during transition period
2. Review and update AML/CFT programs
3. Implement Travel Rule capabilities
4. Segregate customer assets
5. Establish complaint handling procedures

### Ongoing Compliance
1. Quarterly reporting to CMB
2. Annual AML/CFT audit
3. Continuous transaction monitoring
4. Staff training requirements
