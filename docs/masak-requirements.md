# MASAK AML/CFT Requirements for CASPs

*Mali Suçları Araştırma Kurulu (Financial Crimes Investigation Board)*

## Overview

MASAK is Turkey's Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU), responsible for combating money laundering and terrorist financing. All Crypto Asset Service Providers (CASPs) operating in Turkey must comply with MASAK's AML/CFT requirements.

## Legal Framework

### Primary Legislation
- **Law No. 5549** - Prevention of Laundering Proceeds of Crime (2006)
- **Law No. 6415** - Prevention of Financing of Terrorism (2013)
- **Law No. 7518** - Crypto Asset Service Providers (2024)

### Key Regulations
- **Regulation on Prevention of Laundering Proceeds of Crime**
- **MASAK General Communiqué No. 5** (as amended)
- **Circular No. 29** (June 2025) - Crypto-specific guidance

## Obligated Entities

CASPs are classified as **financial institutions** under Turkish AML law, requiring:
- Full compliance with obligated entity requirements
- Direct relationship with MASAK
- Appointment of Compliance Officer

## Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### Standard CDD Requirements

#### Natural Persons
- Full legal name
- Turkish ID number (TC Kimlik No) or passport number
- Date and place of birth
- Current address
- Telephone and email
- Occupation and employer
- Source of funds declaration

#### Legal Entities
- Official company name and registration
- Tax identification number
- Legal structure and ownership
- Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs) - 10% threshold
- Business activities
- Authorized signatories

### Verification Methods
1. **In-Person Verification** - Original documents
2. **Video Verification** - Real-time video call with document display
3. **Electronic Verification** - e-Devlet (government portal) integration

### Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Triggers
- Transactions >500,000 TRY
- High-risk countries (FATF lists)
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- Complex corporate structures
- Unusual transaction patterns
- Turkey-Iran/Russia corridor indicators

## Transaction Monitoring

### Automated Monitoring Required
- All transactions above thresholds
- Pattern detection (structuring, layering)
- Velocity monitoring
- Peer group analysis

### Red Flag Indicators
1. **Structuring** - Multiple transactions just below thresholds
2. **Rapid Movement** - Quick deposits and withdrawals
3. **Mixing Services** - Use of tumbler/mixer addresses
4. **High-Risk Jurisdictions** - Iran, Russia, DPRK connections
5. **Privacy Coins** - XMR, ZEC transactions
6. **Newly Created Accounts** - Large transactions on new accounts

## Reporting Obligations

### Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR)

**When to Report:**
- Knowledge or suspicion of ML/TF
- Unusual transaction patterns without legitimate explanation
- Customer behavior inconsistent with profile
- Connections to sanctioned entities

**Timeline:**
- Report to MASAK within **10 business days**
- Immediate report for terrorism-related suspicions

**Content Requirements:**
- Customer identification information
- Transaction details
- Reason for suspicion
- Supporting documentation
- Compliance officer assessment

### Large Transaction Reports

| Transaction Type | Threshold (TRY) | Deadline |
|------------------|-----------------|----------|
| Wire Transfer | 75,000 | Same day |
| Cash Transaction | 100,000 | Same day |
| Crypto (cumulative) | 75,000 | Same day |

## Travel Rule (Effective February 2025)

### Threshold
- Transactions ≥15,000 TRY (~$440 USD)

### Required Information - Originator
- Full name
- Account number/wallet address
- Physical address OR national ID OR date/place of birth

### Required Information - Beneficiary
- Full name
- Account number/wallet address

### Obligations
1. Collect required information before transfer
2. Transmit information to receiving CASP
3. Reject transfers without required information
4. Maintain records for 8 years

## Circular No. 29 (June 2025)

### Withdrawal Delays
- CASPs may implement withdrawal delays for:
  - First-time large withdrawals
  - Withdrawals to unverified addresses
  - Risk-flagged customers

### Stablecoin Limits
- Enhanced monitoring for stablecoin transactions
- Additional CDD for large stablecoin movements
- Specific reporting for USDT, USDC concentrations

## Record Keeping

### Retention Period
- **8 years** from end of relationship or transaction

### Required Records
- Customer identification documents
- Transaction records
- Correspondence
- Risk assessments
- STRs filed
- Training records

## Compliance Program

### Required Elements
1. Written AML/CFT policies and procedures
2. Board-approved risk assessment
3. Designated Compliance Officer
4. Employee training program
5. Independent audit function
6. Technology systems for monitoring

### Compliance Officer Requirements
- Senior management level
- Direct reporting to board
- Independence from business units
- Adequate resources and authority

## Sanctions

### Administrative
- Fines: 50,000 - 500,000 TRY per violation
- Compliance orders
- Business restrictions

### Criminal
- Referral for prosecution
- Personal liability for compliance failures
- License revocation
